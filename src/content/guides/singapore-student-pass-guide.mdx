## Who Needs a Student's Pass?

Whether your child needs a Student's Pass depends on their immigration status in Singapore:

| Child's Immigration Status | Student's Pass Required? |
|---|---|
| **Dependant's Pass (DP)** holder | ❌ No — DP allows study |
| **Long-Term Visit Pass (LTVP)** holder | ⚠️ Depends on school type |
| **Tourist / Short-Term Visit Pass** | ✅ Yes — required |
| **No Singapore visa yet** | ✅ Yes — required before starting school |

**Most common scenario**: A child of an Employment Pass holder enters Singapore on a Dependant's Pass and can study without a separate Student's Pass. However, some international schools still require the Student's Pass for administrative reasons — check with your school.

## Step-by-Step: Student's Pass Application

### Step 1: School Registers You with ICA

Your international school initiates the process. Under Singapore's Immigration Regulations, **the school must be a registered educational institution with ICA** to sponsor Student's Pass applications. All international schools on this site are ICA-registered.

The school submits:
- Student's personal particulars
- Course details and duration
- School's sponsorship of the application

### Step 2: Submit Documents Online

You'll receive login credentials for ICA's **SOLAR+ (Student's Pass Online Application & Registration)** system. Required documents:

- **Passport** — Valid for at least 6 months beyond the course end date
- **Birth certificate** — Must be in English; if not, provide a certified translation
- **Passport-size photograph** — ICA specifications (35mm x 45mm, white background)
- **Vaccination records** — Required for children under 12; must meet Singapore's National Childhood Immunisation Schedule
- **Parents' passport copies** — For verification
- **School offer letter** — Confirming enrollment

### Step 3: ICA Processing

| Phase | Timeline |
|-------|----------|
| Document submission | Day 1 |
| ICA acknowledgment | 1-3 working days |
| Processing | 2-4 weeks |
| In-Principle Approval (IPA) | If approved |
| Collect Student's Pass card | Within 1 month of IPA |

### Step 4: In-Principle Approval (IPA) Letter

If approved, ICA issues an **IPA letter** — a provisional approval valid for entry into Singapore. The IPA letter allows the student to:
- Enter Singapore (if applying from overseas)
- Begin attending school while waiting for the physical card

### Step 5: Collect the Student's Pass Card

Within the timeframe specified in the IPA letter (typically within 1 month):
1. Complete the medical examination (if required)
2. Book an appointment at ICA Building (10 Kallang Road)
3. Attend the appointment with the student and original documents
4. Provide biometric data (photo + fingerprints for students aged 6+)
5. Receive the physical Student's Pass card

## Student's Pass Validity and Renewal

- Initial validity: Typically aligned with the course duration or 2 years (whichever is shorter)
- Renewal: Must be initiated **at least 2 months before expiry** through your school

### What Happens If the Student's Pass Expires?

- You have a **7-day grace period** after expiry
- After 7 days, the student is considered an overstayer — this has serious immigration consequences
- **Renew early** — ICA processing can take 4-6 weeks during peak periods

## Changing Schools

If your child transfers to a different school:

1. **Cancel the current Student's Pass** — Your current school initiates cancellation with ICA
2. **Receive a 30-day Short-Term Visit Pass** — Covers the transition period
3. **New school applies for a fresh Student's Pass** — Same process as the original application

You cannot simply "transfer" a Student's Pass between schools — each is tied to a specific institution.

## Special Cases

### Boarding School Students

Boarding school students must have a local guardian. The guardian must be:
- A Singapore Citizen or Permanent Resident aged 21+
- Willing to accept legal responsibility for the student

### Students Under 12

Additional vaccination requirements apply. Singapore's National Childhood Immunisation Schedule requires:
- Diphtheria, Tetanus, Pertussis (DTaP)
- Poliomyelitis (IPV/OPV)
- Measles, Mumps, Rubella (MMR)
- Hepatitis B
- Pneumococcal (PCV)
- Varicella (chickenpox) — or proof of past infection

Missing vaccinations can delay or block Student's Pass approval. Check with your paediatrician **before arriving in Singapore.**

### Mother Tongue Students Taking Local MOE Exams

MOE requires all students in mainstream schools to take a Mother Tongue Language exam. International students with Student's Pass who are not from Chinese, Malay, or Tamil language backgrounds may apply for an exemption through their school.

## Common Problems and Solutions

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Application taking too long | Contact your school's admissions office — they can check status with ICA |
| IPA letter received but travel plans changed | IPA letters are typically valid for 6 months |
| Medical exam flagged an issue | Contact ICA immediately; some conditions are waivable |
| Lost Student's Pass card | Report to ICA immediately; replacement fee ~$100 |
| Pass expiring during school holidays | Renew before the holiday — don't wait until you return |

## Quick Checklist Before Applying

- [ ] Passport valid for 6+ months beyond course end date
- [ ] Birth certificate in English (or certified translation)
- [ ] Passport photo meeting ICA specifications
- [ ] All childhood vaccinations up to date per Singapore schedule
- [ ] School offer letter received
- [ ] Application fee ready (SGD $30)
- [ ] Medical examination scheduled (if required)
- [ ] Local guardian arranged (if boarding)
