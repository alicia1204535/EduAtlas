## The Real Cost of International Schools in Singapore

Singapore is home to some of the world's best — and most expensive — international schools. Annual tuition ranges from approximately **SGD 15,000 to over SGD 50,000** per child, not including the numerous additional costs that can add 20–40% to the headline figure.

This guide breaks down every cost you'll face, from application to graduation.

## The Hidden Costs: What Tuition Doesn't Cover

### One-Time Fees (Upfront)

| Cost Item | Typical Range (SGD) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Application Fee | 300 – 1,000 | Non-refundable. Payable per child. |
| Enrolment / Registration Fee | 2,000 – 10,000 | One-time, non-refundable. SAS: $9,630. UWCSEA: $8,800. |
| Confirmation Deposit | 1,000 – 5,000 | Usually refundable against final term fees. |
| Building / Capital Levy | 0 – 5,000/year | Some schools charge this annually or as one lump sum. |

Total one-time costs to start at a premium school: **SGD 5,000 – 20,000+**

### Annual Recurring Costs

| Cost Item | Typical Range (SGD/year) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Uniforms | 300 – 800 | PE kit, daily uniform, shoes |
| School Lunch | 800 – 2,000 | Some schools include in fees |
| Transport (School Bus) | 2,000 – 4,500 | Depends on distance |
| Technology / Device | 500 – 2,000 | Laptops, iPads; some schools include |
| Field Trips / Camps | 500 – 3,000 | Increases with grade level |
| After-School Activities | 500 – 3,000 | Varies widely by activity |
| Examinations (IGCSE/IB/AP) | 500 – 2,000 | Exam registration fees |

Annual additional costs: **SGD 5,000 – 15,000 per child**

## Money-Saving Strategies

### 1. Employer Education Allowance
Many expatriate packages include an education allowance. Typical coverage: SGD 20,000–$50,000 per child per year. Negotiate this as part of your relocation package.

### 2. Sibling Discounts
Several schools offer discounts for multiple children enrolled:
- Tanglin Trust: Discount for 3rd+ child
- Dulwich College: Sibling discount available
- Always ask — even schools without published policies may consider it

### 3. Consider Value-Tier Schools
Schools like Invictus, OWIS, and Middleton offer internationally recognized curricula (IPC, Cambridge, IGCSE) at roughly half the cost of premium schools. The trade-off is typically in facilities and extracurricular breadth.

### 4. Apply to Local Schools (Limited)
International students can apply to Singapore's mainstream schools through the AEIS exam. Annual fees: SGD 5,280–$16,800 (primary), $8,400–$22,800 (secondary). Competition is intense and places are limited.

### 5. Plan Your School Years
Avoid mid-year transitions where possible — you may pay a full year's fees for a partial year. Most schools align their academic year starting in August.
