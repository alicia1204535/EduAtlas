## Introduction: Why Curriculum Choice Matters

Choosing a curriculum is arguably more important than choosing a school. The curriculum determines _how_ your child learns, _what_ they learn, _how_ they're assessed, and ultimately _which universities_ will recognize their qualifications.

Singapore's international schools offer four major curriculum pathways:

| Curriculum | Origin | Age Range | Schools in SG |
|---|---|---|---|
| **IB** (PYP, MYP, DP) | International (Switzerland) | 3–19 | 25+ |
| **IGCSE** | United Kingdom | 14–16 (Years 10–11) | 20+ |
| **AP** (Advanced Placement) | United States | 16–18 (Grades 10–12) | 5+ |
| **A-Levels** | United Kingdom | 16–18 (Years 12–13) | 10+ |

This guide breaks down each curriculum in detail, comparing teaching philosophy, assessment methods, university recognition, and — most importantly — which type of learner each suits best.

## The IB (International Baccalaureate)

### What Is the IB?

The IB is a holistic, internationally recognized educational framework headquartered in Geneva, Switzerland. It comprises four programmes, but the three most relevant to international schools are:

- **IB PYP** (Primary Years Programme): Ages 3–12. Inquiry-based, transdisciplinary learning.
- **IB MYP** (Middle Years Programme): Ages 11–16. Eight subject groups with interdisciplinary connections.
- **IB DP** (Diploma Programme): Ages 16–19. Six subjects + Theory of Knowledge (TOK) + Extended Essay (EE) + Creativity, Activity, Service (CAS).

### Teaching Style

Inquiry-driven. Students learn by asking questions, researching, and constructing understanding. Teachers act as facilitators rather than lecturers. The IB emphasizes _how_ to think, not _what_ to think.

### Assessment

- **Internal assessments** throughout the programme.
- **External exams** at the end of the Diploma (scored 1–7 per subject, max 45 points including 3 bonus points from TOK + EE).
- **No single correct answer** — essays and open-ended problems are common.

### University Recognition

Universally recognized. Over 5,000 universities in 100+ countries accept the IB Diploma. Particularly strong in:
- UK (including Oxbridge)
- Europe
- Australia
- Canada
- Top US universities (often with advanced standing/credit for high scores)

### Best For

- Well-rounded students who thrive in multiple subjects
- Students who enjoy writing, research, and independent projects
- Families who may relocate internationally
- Students applying to universities globally (not just one country)

### IB Schools in Singapore (Examples)

- UWC South East Asia (Dover & East)
- Canadian International School (CIS)
- Nexus International School
- XCL World Academy
- Chatsworth International School

## IGCSE (International General Certificate of Secondary Education)

### What Is IGCSE?

IGCSE is a British qualification typically taken at age 16 (Years 10–11), developed by Cambridge Assessment International Education or Pearson Edexcel. It's the world's most popular international qualification for 14–16 year olds.

### Teaching Style

More structured and content-focused than IB. Subjects are taught individually with clear syllabi and defined outcomes. Students typically take 6–10 subjects.

### Assessment

- Primarily **external examinations** at the end of the two-year course.
- Some subjects include coursework or practical assessments.
- Graded A\* to G (Cambridge) or 9–1 (Edexcel).
- Linear assessment — results depend heavily on final exam performance.

### Best For

- Students preparing for A-Levels or IB Diploma afterwards
- Students who prefer clear structure and defined content
- Strong exam performers
- Families on a British educational pathway

### Key Consideration

IGCSEs alone are not a university entrance qualification — they are a _stepping stone_ to A-Levels, IB Diploma, or equivalent pre-university programmes.

## AP (Advanced Placement)

### What Is AP?

The Advanced Placement programme, developed by the US College Board, offers university-level courses in high school (Grades 10–12). Students can take individual AP courses in subjects of interest; there is no mandatory set of subjects. There are 38 AP courses available.

### Teaching Style

Subject-specific, content-heavy courses similar to introductory university classes. Emphasizes mastery of factual knowledge and analytical skills within individual disciplines.

### Assessment

- **Single external exam** per subject each May, scored 1–5.
- Most exams are a mix of multiple-choice and free-response.
- Scores of 3+ are generally considered "passing" and may earn university credit.

### University Recognition

- Primarily recognized by **US and Canadian universities**.
- Many US universities grant course credit or advanced placement for scores of 4 or 5.
- Less recognized by UK, European, or Australian universities (though changing).

### Best For

- Students targeting US universities
- Students who excel in specific subjects (can load APs in strengths while taking fewer in weaker areas)
- Students who perform well on standardized tests
- Self-directed learners (can self-study APs outside of school)

### AP Schools in Singapore (Examples)

- Singapore American School (SAS) — 25+ AP courses
- Stamford American International School (SAIS)
- ICS Singapore

## A-Levels

### What Are A-Levels?

A-Levels (Advanced Level qualifications) are the traditional British pre-university qualification, typically taken at ages 16–18 (Years 12–13). Students specialize in 3–4 subjects studied in depth over two years.

### Teaching Style

Deep, specialized, and academically rigorous. Students focus exclusively on their chosen subjects. A-Level Mathematics, for example, covers more advanced content than IB Higher Level Mathematics.

### Assessment

- Primarily **external examinations** at the end of the two-year course.
- Graded A\* to E.
- Some subjects include coursework components.
- Results depend almost entirely on final exams.

### University Recognition

- **Gold standard** for UK universities (required for all Russell Group universities).
- Widely accepted in Commonwealth countries (Australia, New Zealand, Singapore).
- Accepted by US universities (often with credit for high grades).
- Accepted by European universities.

### Best For

- Students who know their academic strengths and career direction early
- Students targeting UK universities
- Students who prefer deep specialization over breadth
- Strong exam performers who don't mind high-stakes testing

### A-Level Schools in Singapore (Examples)

- Tanglin Trust School (offers both A-Levels and IB DP)
- Dulwich College (Singapore)
- Brighton College (Singapore)

## Side-by-Side Comparison

| Dimension | IB Diploma | AP | A-Levels | IGCSE (stepping stone) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Subjects** | 6 required (broad) | Flexible (any number) | 3–4 (specialized) | 6–10 |
| **Teaching style** | Inquiry-based | Content-heavy | Deep & specialized | Structured |
| **Assessment** | Exams + coursework + essays | Single exam per subject | Mainly exams | Mainly exams |

| **University prep** | Global | US-focused | UK-focused | Pre-A-Level/IB |
| **Best for** | Well-rounded learners | Subject specialists (US) | Deep specialists | Middle years foundation |

## How to Choose: A Parent's Decision Framework

### Ask These Questions

1. **Where might your child go to university?**  
   - US → AP or IB  
   - UK → A-Levels or IB  
   - Global → IB (most portable)

2. **How does your child learn best?**  
   - Enjoys projects, essays, open-ended questions → IB  
   - Prefers clear structure, exams, right/wrong answers → A-Levels or AP  
   - Undecided → IB provides the broadest foundation

3. **Does your child have clear strengths?**  
   - Strong in everything → IB  
   - Clear STEM/humanities preference → A-Levels (specialize deeper)  
   - Wants flexibility → AP (choose subjects à la carte)

4. **How mobile is your family?**  
   - Likely to relocate → IB (most transferable) or British system (IGCSE → A-Levels, available globally)  
   - Staying in Singapore → any curriculum works

5. **What's your budget?**  
   - IB schools tend to be at the higher end (SGD 35,000–$50,000/year)  
   - British-curriculum schools span the full range (SGD 20,000–$48,000/year)  
   - AP schools are fewer but comparable to IB pricing
