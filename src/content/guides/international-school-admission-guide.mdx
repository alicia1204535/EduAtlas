## The Singapore International School Admission Landscape

Applying to an international school in Singapore can feel overwhelming — especially if you're relocating, unfamiliar with the system, or facing waitlists at popular schools. This guide walks you through the entire process, from initial research to your child's first day.

---

## Step 1: Research and Shortlist (6–18 Months Before)

### Determine Your Priorities

Rank these factors for your family:

1. **Curriculum**: IB, British (IGCSE/A-Levels), American (AP), Australian, Indian, or other?
2. **Location**: Which part of Singapore will you live in? Commute times matter.
3. **Budget**: Total cost including all hidden fees (see our [fees guide](/guides/singapore-school-fees-breakdown))
4. **School size and culture**: Large (2,500+ students) vs. small (under 1,000)? Competitive vs. nurturing?
5. **Special requirements**: Learning support, English language support (EAL/ESL), mother tongue programmes?

### Build Your Shortlist

Use our [school finder](/schools) to filter by curriculum, region, and fee range. Aim for 5–7 schools covering different tiers:

- 2–3 **reach schools** (popular, may have waitlists)
- 2–3 **target schools** (good fit, reasonable availability)
- 1–2 **safety schools** (higher availability, easier admission)

---

## Step 2: Contact Schools and Visit Campuses (6–12 Months Before)

### What to Do

- **Contact admissions offices**: Confirm availability for your child's age group and expected start date.
- **Schedule campus tours**: Most schools offer individual tours or open houses. Visit during a school day to see real teaching and learning.
- **Prepare questions**: Ask about class sizes, curriculum details, after-school activities, EAL support, and university counseling.

### Questions to Ask on a Campus Visit

1. What is the current availability for my child's grade?
2. Is there a waitlist? If so, how long is it?
3. What does the entrance assessment involve?
4. What EAL/ESL support do you offer?
5. What is the typical class size?
6. How do you handle transitions for new students?
7. What after-school activities are included in the fees?

---

## Step 3: Prepare Your Application (3–6 Months Before)

### Required Documents (Typical)

- Completed online application form
- Child's birth certificate (translated to English if needed)
- Passport copy (child and parents)
- Recent passport-size photograph
- School reports/transcripts from the last 2–3 years
- Confidential teacher recommendation(s) — usually 1–2
- Immunization records
- Application fee payment (SGD 300–$1,000, non-refundable)

### For Students with Additional Needs

- Educational psychologist reports
- IEP (Individualized Education Plan) from current school
- Specialist assessments (speech, occupational therapy, etc.)

### Application Deadlines

| Intake Month | Apply By | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| August (main intake) | February–April | Most common. Largest number of spaces available. |
| January (mid-year) | September–October | Fewer spaces. Some schools don't offer January entry. |
| Rolling | Any time | Some schools accept applications year-round. |

**Pro tip**: Apply as early as possible. Popular schools like UWCSEA, Tanglin Trust, and SAS often fill places 12–18 months in advance for certain grade levels.

## Step 4: Entrance Assessments and Interviews (2–4 Months Before)

### Common Assessment Types

| Age Group | Typical Assessment |
|---|---|
| Early Years (2–5) | Play-based observation, developmental readiness |
| Primary (6–11) | English & Mathematics tests, informal interview |
| Middle/High (12+) | English & Mathematics tests, formal interview, possibly subject-specific tests |

### English Language Requirements

Most schools require grade-level English proficiency. If your child's English is below the required level:

- **EAL/ESL programmes**: Many schools offer in-class or pull-out English support (usually at additional cost).
- **Some schools are more flexible**: OFS, ICS, and some mid-tier schools have strong EAL programmes.
- **Premium schools are strict**: UWCSEA, Tanglin Trust, SAS generally require high English proficiency.

### Interview Tips

- Be honest about your child's strengths and challenges
- Show genuine interest in the school's values and community
- Let your child speak for themselves (schools want to see the child, not just the parents)

---

## Step 5: Offer, Acceptance, and Payment (1–3 Months Before)

### If You Receive an Offer

1. Review the offer letter and fee schedule carefully
2. Pay the enrolment fee and deposit (SGD 2,000–$10,000+)
3. Sign and return the acceptance form
4. Note the deadline — offers typically expire within 2–4 weeks

### If You're Waitlisted

- Ask your position on the waitlist (some schools will tell you, others won't)
- Ask whether spaces typically open up
- Stay in touch with the admissions office (but don't harass them)
- Have a backup plan — secure a place at another school

### Priority Categories (Most Schools)

1. Siblings of currently enrolled students
2. Children of alumni
3. Certain nationalities (varies by school — e.g., US citizens at SAS, British citizens at Tanglin Trust)
4. Corporate/embassy-sponsored families

---

## Step 6: Student Pass and Relocation (1–2 Months Before)

### Student Pass (International Students)

International students must hold a valid Student's Pass to study in Singapore:

1. **School applies for the Student's Pass** on your behalf after you accept the offer
2. **ICA (Immigration & Checkpoints Authority)** processes the application (2–4 weeks)
3. Required documents: Passport, birth certificate, vaccination records, passport photo

**Note**: Dependant's Pass holders (children of Employment Pass holders) can usually study without a separate Student's Pass — check with your school.

### Before the First Day

- Order uniforms (allow 2–4 weeks for delivery)
- Arrange school transport (book early — bus routes fill up)
- Set up school communication apps/portals
- Attend orientation sessions
- Connect with the parent community (Facebook groups, WhatsApp chats)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Applying to only one school** — Always have backups, especially for popular grade levels.
2. **Waiting too long to apply** — Popular schools fill 12–18 months ahead. Apply as soon as you know you're moving.
3. **Ignoring the hidden costs** — budget 20–40% above headline tuition for additional fees and expenses.
4. **Not visiting the campus** — A website can't convey school culture. Visit during school hours if possible.
5. **Choosing a school based on reputation alone** — The "best" school on paper may not be the best fit for your child.
