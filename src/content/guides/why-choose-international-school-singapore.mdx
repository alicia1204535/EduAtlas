## Why Singapore Is Asia's International Education Hub

Singapore has established itself as the premier destination for international education in Asia. With over **40 international schools** offering curricula from British, American, Australian, Indian, French, German, and International Baccalaureate (IB) systems, the city-state provides unparalleled choice for families.

But what makes Singapore truly special is the **quality and regulation** of its education ecosystem. All international schools must register with the Ministry of Education (MOE) and meet the stringent requirements of the EduTrust certification scheme, ensuring a baseline of quality that doesn't exist in many neighboring countries.

## International Schools vs. Local Schools: The Key Differences

For expatriate families, international schools are often the **default choice** — but they're not the only option. Here's how they compare to Singapore's local school system:

### Curriculum

- **International Schools**: Offer globally recognized curricula — IB, IGCSE, AP, A-Levels, Australian, Indian, etc. These transfer seamlessly when families relocate.
- **Local Schools**: Follow the Singapore national curriculum, which culminates in the PSLE (Primary 6), GCE O-Levels, and GCE A-Levels. The system is renowned globally for its rigor in Mathematics and Science.

### Language of Instruction

- **International Schools**: English is typically the primary language, with additional language offerings (Mandarin, French, Spanish, etc.).
- **Local Schools**: English is the medium of instruction, but Mother Tongue (Mandarin, Malay, or Tamil) is compulsory. The bilingual policy is a strength but can be challenging for non-native speakers.

### Admission

- **International Schools**: Generally open admission for expatriate students. Some schools have waitlists (UWCSEA, Tanglin Trust, SAS) and selective entrance assessments.
- **Local Schools**: International students can apply through the AEIS (Admissions Exercise for International Students), but places are limited and competitive. Priority goes to Singapore citizens and Permanent Residents.

### Cost

- **International Schools**: SGD 18,000 – $55,000 per year, plus application fees, enrolment fees, uniforms, and activities.
- **Local Schools**: SGD 440 – $800 per month for international students (primary), SGD 700 – $1,400 per month (secondary). Significantly more affordable but with limited availability.

### Cultural Environment

- **International Schools**: Diverse, multinational student body. 40–80+ nationalities represented at most schools. Excellent for families who want their children in a global environment.
- **Local Schools**: Predominantly Singaporean students. Immersive exposure to Singaporean culture and values but may feel less internationally oriented.

## Who Should Choose an International School?

International schools in Singapore are ideal for:

1. **Expatriate families on short to medium-term assignments** — the curriculum portability means children can transfer smoothly when relocating to another country.

2. **Families pursuing specific university pathways** — IB and AP are widely recognized by universities worldwide. A-Levels (British system) are accepted by UK, Australian, and Commonwealth universities.

3. **Children who thrive in inquiry-based learning** — IB schools use student-led, inquiry-driven approaches rather than traditional lecture-based instruction.

4. **Families who value holistic development** — International schools emphasize sports, arts, service learning, and extracurricular activities alongside academics.

5. **Students needing English language support** — Many international schools offer robust EAL (English as an Additional Language) programmes.

## The Hidden Benefits of International Schools in Singapore

Beyond academics, international schools in Singapore offer several underappreciated advantages:

### 1. University Counseling

Top international schools employ dedicated university counselors who guide students through applications to competitive universities worldwide. Schools like UWCSEA, Tanglin Trust, and SAS send graduates to Ivy League, Oxbridge, and top Asian universities annually.

### 2. Extracurricular Breadth

International schools invest heavily in facilities and programmes: Olympic-size pools, performing arts centers, innovation labs, and competitive sports teams. These opportunities develop well-rounded students and build impressive university applications.

### 3. Parent Community

For expat families, the school community often becomes their primary social network. Schools facilitate this through parent associations, events, and volunteer opportunities.

### 4. Pastoral Care and Wellbeing

Singapore's international schools have invested significantly in student wellbeing, with dedicated counselors, mindfulness programmes, and robust safeguarding policies.

## Common Concerns (and Honest Answers)

### "Are international schools worth the cost?"

For most expat families, the answer is yes — especially if an employer covers tuition as part of a relocation package. For families paying out-of-pocket, the decision requires weighing the premium against local or more affordable international options. Our [school comparison tool](/schools/compare) can help you evaluate your options.

### "Will my child fall behind if we return home?"

Most international curricula are designed with mobility in mind. Schools provide detailed transcripts and curriculum maps to facilitate smooth transitions. The IB, in particular, is recognized in over 150 countries.

### "Are there affordable international school options?"

Yes. Schools like **Overseas Family School (OFS)** and **Invictus International School** offer more accessible fee structures (SGD 18,000 – $32,000/year) while maintaining quality international education. Some offer the same IB curriculum as premium schools at a fraction of the cost.
